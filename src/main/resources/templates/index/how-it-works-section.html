Helping neighbors

High risk to virus

If you are a person who is at a higher risk to COVID19, meaning that you are above 60 years of age or you have other underlying health conditions, you are advised to stay inside of your house. To satisfy the need of social distancing, you can receive help from someone in your community, such as help with your groceries, pick up prescriptions, or simply check in every couple of days.

We care deeply about you and want you to remain healthy.

After you sign-up for free to our website, a volunteer from your neighborhood will be able to see that you need help and they will call you in order to establish a connection.

Low risk to virus

If you are a person who is at lower risk to COVID19, meaning that you are under 60 years of age and can help elders or other high-risk neighbors in your community.

Some examples of voluteering may include; picking up groceries, prescriptions, delivering a homemade meal or simply by checking in every couple of days.

After you complete the registration process you will be able to see the people in your area that need your help.

The information will be displayed in two different ways; Distance in miles from your location and an interactive map with unclaimed people who are still in need of your help.
